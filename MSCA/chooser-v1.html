My Service Canada Account

My Service Canada Account (MSCA) is a secure online portal. It allows you to access services for Employment Insurance (EI), Canada Pension Plan (CPP), Canada Pension Plan disability, Old Age Security (OAS) and Social Insurance Number (SIN).

To update your direct deposit information, mailing address or telephone number, you can:

  • complete the eServiceCanada form
  • visit a Service Canada Office, or
  • contact the call centre responsible for the benefits you have received

New: Learn how to access your SIN information in MSCA.

Forgot your GCKey password or username

Forgot your GCKey password or username

Choose the instructions for your situation:

Forgot your username AND password

If you added a recovery email address to GCKey:

  1. Select Forgot your username on the Welcome to GCKey page to have it emailed to you
  2. Once you receive your username through email, select Forgot your password? on the Welcome to GCKey page and follow the steps to reset it
If you didn't add a recovery email address to GCKey:
  1. Select the Sign up on the Welcome to GCKey page to create a new GCKey username and password and register for a new account
  2. During this process you may be able to recover your account or link existing applications to your new account
Forgot your username
  1. Select Forgot your username? on the on the Welcome to GCKey page
  2. Enter your recovery email address on the Recover Username Step 1 of 2 page:
    • you provided your recovery email address either when you signed up for your GCKey username and password or at a later time
    • if you didn't add a recovery email address and have forgotten your username and your password, select the Sign up button on the Welcome to GCKey page to create a new GCKey username and password, and register again
  3. Select Continue
    • You'll be brought to a page that explains where your username will be emailed to and what to do if you don't receive the email
  4. Select Continue again
  5. Check your email, you should receive an email from the Government of Canada with your GCKey username
  6. Sign in with your username and password on the Welcome to GCKey page
Forgot your password
  1. Select Forgot your password? on the Welcome to GCKey page
  2. Enter your GCKey username
  3. Select Continue
    • If you entered an email address when signing up for your GCKey username then you should be able to reset your password by entering that email address
    • If you didn't enter an email address when signing up then follow the instructions in the Recovery Questions and Answers box
Password reset using an email address
  1. If you entered your email address, check your email, you should receive an email from the Government of Canada with a confirmation code
  2. Enter the confirmation code you receive on the Forgot Your Password Step 2 of 4 page (this page should have automatically loaded for you after you entered your username and selected Continue on the Forget Your Password Step 1 page)
  3. Select Continue
  4. You'll be prompted with a recovery question
    • This is a question you would have answered when you first created a GCKey username and password
  5. Enter the answer to the question in the My Recovery Answer field
  6. Select Continue
  7. Enter a new password in the New Password field
  8. Re-enter that same password in the Confirm New Password field
  9. Select Continue
  10. Select Continue again on the Forget Your Password Complete page
Password reset using recovery questions
  1. On the Forget Your Password Step 2 of 4 page, select the Recovery Q&A button in the Recovery Questions and Answers box
  2. Enter your answers in the My Recovery Answer, My Memorable Person Answer and My Memorable Date fields (you should find the questions/hints above each field)
  3. Enter a new password in the Create Your New Password field
  4. Enter the same password in the Confirm Your New Password field
  5. Select Continue
  6. If you correctly answered all the recovery questions you'll be brought to a page called Forget Your Password Complete
  7. Select Continue
GCKey username and password revoked

Your GCKey username and password are revoked, missing, invalid or incomplete

If you haven't used your GCKey username and password at least once in the last 2 years, they have been revoked and removed. You'll need to:

Link your applications to a new account

  1. select the Sign up button to make a new GCKey username and password on the Welcome to GCkey page
  2. follow all the steps to register for a new account
  3. once you've got the new account, if you have applications in progress, select Link application to this account under the heading “What would you like to do today?”
  4. as a security measure you will have to enter specific information about your application
    • have a copy of your application form on hand so that you can enter the information exactly as it appears on your application form
Switch your Sign-in partner

Use a different Sign-in partner

If you've moved to a new bank or credit union, and already sign in through Interac® Sign-in Partner, you may be able to switch:

  • select Switch My Sign-in Partner from the menu bar on the Interac® Sign-in Partnerpage

How to protect your personal information
  • Be aware of your surroundings when you enter passwords and security codes in public
  • Don't share your passwords or security codes
  • Sign out of MSCA when you are done, then close your browser
  • Learn how Multi-factor authentication helps protect your personal information
What is a Personal Access Code (PAC)

A personal Access Code (PAC), is a way to help you verify your identity

If you choose to register for MSCA using GCKey, you will need a PAC

You need to submit a request in order to receive a PAC. Wait time varies depending on whether you choose to request a PAC online, by mail, or in person.

  • Request a PAC
  • Get help finding your access code
Avoid technical problems
  • Don't bookmark secure MSCA pages (which means the pages you see after you sign in)
  • Use one of the following Web browsers:
    • Google Chrome
    • Safari
    • Microsoft Edge
    • Mozilla Firefox
  • For Apple users, use one of the following operating system:
    • iOS (version 13 or newer)
    • MacOS (version 10.15 or newer)
  • Enable cookies and JavaScript in your browser
