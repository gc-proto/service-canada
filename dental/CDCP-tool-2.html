Canadian Dental Care Plan

The Canadian Dental Care Plan (CDCP) will help cover some of the cost of various medically necessary dental care services for Canadian residents who qualify.

The initial application process will open progressively, increasing the number of applicants between December 2023 and 2025.

Parents of children under the age of 12

If you are a parent or guardian of a child under the age of 12 and do not have dental insurance, you may be eligible for the Canada Dental Benefit, which is available now.

What is adjusted family net income
  • Your 2022 family net income (line 23600 of your tax return plus line 23600 of your spouse's or common-law partner's tax return, and any world income not reported in a 2022 tax return to the CRA, such as by a new resident)
  • minus Any universal child care benefit (UCCB) and registered disability savings plan (RDSP) income received in 2022 (line 11700 and line 12500 of your or your spouse's tax return)
  • plus Any UCCB and RDSP amounts repaid in 2022 (line 21300 and line 23200 of your or your spouse's tax return)
  • equals 2022 adjusted family net income

How the application periods will be rolled out

The initial application period will be rolled out according to different start dates set for each group.

Group 1: Seniors
  • Seniors aged 77 and older: December 2023
  • Seniors between the age of 70 and 76: January 2024
  • Seniors between the age of 65 and 70: February 2024
Group 2: People with a valid DTC and children under 18
  • Adults between 18 and 64 years with a disability who have a valid Disability Tax Credit certificate : May 2024
  • Children under the age of 18: May 2024
Group 3: All remaining eligible Canadians

All remaining eligible Canadians can apply in 2025.

How do I apply

Potential applicants have been identified based on the eligibility criteria. Those who qualify before May 2024 will be mailed a personalized invitation with the instructions on how to apply.

Only those who receive a letter with a unique application code and will be able to apply at this time.

If you received a letter to apply

  1. Follow the instructions in the invitation.
  2. Wait to receive a confirmation that application qualifies for the CDCP. The CDCP will share the applicant’s information with the third-party benefits administrator.
  3. Wait to receive the welcome package for new members, including information on the CDCP, a membership card and the member’s start date for coverage.

After May 2024, a Services Canada portal will be available to apply online. More details will be provided once available.

What does the CDCP cover

What is covered

The CDCP covers dental services that help prevent and treat oral disease and the consequences of oral disease, such as (this list is non-exhaustive):

  • diagnostic services, including examinations and X-rays
  • preventive services, including scaling (cleaning), polishing, sealants and fluoride
  • restorative services, including fillings and crowns
  • endodontic services, including root canal treatments
  • periodontal services, including deep scaling
  • oral surgery services, including extractions

When will services be covered

The date when services will be covered is unique to each applicant and based on when the applicant is confirmed to qualify for the CDCP. Once confirmed, an applicant becomes a member and will receive welcome package that will include the date their coverage begins.

The earliest members can anticipate that their coverage may begin is April 2024, however individual start dates are determined by when the applicant applies.

Dental services obtained before a member’s confirmed start date will not be covered.

Qualifying CDCP members will be reassessed annually

CDCP members who qualify will be reassessed annually to confirm they still meet the eligibility criteria. More information on the process will be provided to members.

How much will be covered

The CDCP aims to provide financial support to Canadians who do not have access to dental insurance, to get the dental care they need.

To limit the out-of-pocket expenses for those who qualify under the plan, oral health providers who agree to participate in the CDCP and provide dental care services, will be required to bill the CDCP directly for reimbursement and not the member.

CDCP members may be required to pay a portion of the cost when visiting an oral health professional. The CDCP will reimburse a percentage of any eligible expense. The percentage that is not covered by the CDCP and that you will have to pay is called the co-payment.

The table below shows percentage of co-payments depending on the adjusted family net income:

Co-payments based on adjusted family net income
AFNI income Co-payment coverage How much you pay
Lower than $70,000 Up to 100% of expenses will be covered for dental care services. 0%
Between $70,000 and $79,999 60% of expenses will be covered for dental care services. 40%
Between $80,000 and $89,999 40% of expenses will be covered for dental care services. 60%

The CDCP will only pay for dental care services covered within the plan and in accordance with its approved rates.

How oral health professionals register to be dental care providers

Oral health providers are an important partner in the success of the CDCP. They will be invited to participate and enrol in the CDCP as dental care providers directly through Sun Life, who will be administrating the benefits claims for the CDCP. Provider enrolment should begin in early 2024 and more information will be available soon.

While applications for eligible CDCP members will open in December 2023, services covered under the plan will only begin in April 2024.
